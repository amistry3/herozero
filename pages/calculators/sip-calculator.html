About SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

A SIP is a simple way to invest a fixed amount regularly into a mutual fund. Instead of putting a large sum at once, you invest small amounts (for example, every month). Over time this helps you build savings while keeping your investments disciplined.

How a SIP works

Each time you invest, you buy units of the chosen fund at that day's price (NAV). When prices are low you buy more units, and when prices are high you buy fewer units — this evens out your average cost over time. Your investment grows through compounding: gains are reinvested, and they can earn returns too.

Why people use SIPs

  • Starts small: you can begin with modest monthly amounts.
  • Builds discipline: regular investing becomes a habit.
  • Smooths market swings: buying at different prices lowers risk of bad timing.
  • Benefits from compounding: reinvested returns help the value grow over the long run.
  • Flexible: most funds let you pause, increase, decrease or stop your SIP.

Types of SIPs (short)

  • Regular SIP: same amount at fixed intervals.
  • Step-up / Top-up SIP: amount increases automatically at set times.
  • Flexible SIP: you can change the amount based on your cash flow.
  • Trigger / Perpetual SIP: starts or continues based on market or time triggers, or runs indefinitely until stopped.

Quick FAQs

How much can I invest?
There is usually no strict upper limit; minimums vary by fund (many start around ₹500/month).
How long should I stay invested?
There is no fixed maximum. For equity funds, staying for several years helps ride out short-term ups and downs.
Can I change or pause my SIP?
Yes — most funds let you increase, decrease, pause or stop your SIP when needed.
