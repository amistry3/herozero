Understanding Expense Ratios

An expense ratio is the annual percentage of fund assets used for administrative, management, and distribution fees. It is deducted before returns reach you. Two funds with the same gross performance but different fees will diverge more each year due to compounding.

How we compute values

For each scenario we take the gross annual return (before fees) you enter, subtract the expense ratio to arrive at a net rate, then compound: Valuey+1 = Valuey × (1 + (Gross − Expense)).

The difference after N years is the cumulative fee drag (not just the fees paid in year N).

Why small gaps matter

A half‑percent annual difference over long horizons can erase lakhs in final value. The earlier you switch to a lower-cost option, the more compounding works in your favour.

Focus on net returns you keep, not only on past performance charts.

Assumptions & Notes
  • Gross return is constant (simplification). Real markets vary.
  • Compounded annually; intra‑year flows/market volatility ignored.
  • Taxes, exit loads, tracking errors not included.
  • Use as an educational comparison, not investment advice.
