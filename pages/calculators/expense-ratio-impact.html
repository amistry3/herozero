Expense Ratio Impact

See how different expense ratios change final returns over time.

Why expense ratio matters

Expense ratio is an annual fee charged by a mutual fund to cover operating costs. Even small differences compound over time and can materially change your final returns. This calculator shows two scenarios side-by-side: the same gross return but different expense ratios. The lower the expense ratio, the more of the return stays with you.

How this calculator works

  1. We start with your initial investment.
  2. We apply the gross annual return you enter, then subtract the expense ratio to get the net annual return for each fund.
  3. We compound the net return each year for the duration to compute final values.

Why it impacts your investment

Even a 0.5% higher expense ratio can reduce your terminal value significantly over long periods. Use the chart to compare growth trajectories and the table to see absolute differences.
