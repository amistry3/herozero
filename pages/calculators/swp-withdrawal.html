SWP / Withdrawal Planner

Estimate how long a corpus lasts given a periodic withdrawal.

About this SWP / Withdrawal Planner

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) lets you withdraw a fixed amount from a corpus at regular intervals while the remainder continues to earn returns. This planner estimates how long your corpus will last if you withdraw a constant monthly amount and the remaining corpus earns a steady annual return.

How it works

  1. We simulate the corpus month-by-month: each month the remaining corpus grows by the monthly portion of the annual return, then the withdrawal is taken out.
  2. The simulation stops when the corpus falls to zero (depleted) or after a safe cap of months.
  3. We present the number of months the corpus lasts, a year-by-year chart of the remaining corpus, and a table of year-end balances so you can visualise longevity.

Why it matters

This helps you choose a sustainable withdrawal amount and plan for retirement income. If withdrawals exceed the sustainable limit, the corpus will deplete; if withdrawals are conservative, the corpus can last many years or indefinitely.
