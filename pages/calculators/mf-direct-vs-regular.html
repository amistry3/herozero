Compare Mutual Fund: Direct vs Regular

Quickly estimate how a difference in expense ratio between Direct and Regular plans affects your returns.

How to decide: Direct vs Regular

Direct plans generally have lower expense ratios because they exclude distributor commissions; over long horizons, even small differences compound into sizeable value. Regular plans may include distribution/support services or allow investing through certain platforms with bundled benefits.

Key points to consider

  • Expense ratio: Primary driver of difference — lower ER favors Direct over long horizons.
  • Transaction & platform fees: Some brokers charge fees for Direct plan purchases; include those when comparing.
  • Convenience & advice: If you value relationship/advice, regular plans through advisors may be preferred despite higher ER.
  • Investment size & horizon: For small, short-term investments the difference may be negligible; for long-term SIPs and large lumpsums, prefer lower ER (Direct) where possible.
  • Exit & taxation: Tax treatment is the same for Direct and Regular; treat them as the same fund for holding-period rules.

Practical checklist

  1. Compare the net expense (ER + any platform or transaction fees).
  2. Run a small test investment and monitor service/support quality.
  3. If you have tools and prefer lower costs, choose Direct; if you need offline advisor services and accept the cost, Regular may be okay.
