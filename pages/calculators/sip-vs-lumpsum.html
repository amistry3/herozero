SIP vs Lumpsum — Simple Guide

Both SIP and lumpsum are ways to invest in the same funds. The main difference is timing: with lumpsum you invest one big amount at once; with SIP you invest smaller amounts regularly (for example, monthly).

Which one is better?

There is no one-size-fits-all answer. Lumpsum can grow faster if markets rise soon after you invest. SIP helps if markets are volatile because it spreads purchases over time and lowers the risk of bad timing.

When to prefer SIP

  • You don’t have a large amount right now.
  • You want to build a saving habit.
  • You prefer lower risk from market timing.

When lumpsum can make sense

  • You have a large idle amount and can tolerate short-term market swings.
  • You believe markets are likely to rise soon (note: timing is hard).

Quick tips

  • Compare after-tax returns and fund costs before deciding.
  • Combining both approaches is common: invest a lumpsum when available and set up SIPs for steady savings.
