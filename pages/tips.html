Practical Tips to Save Money and Make Better Financial Decisions

Short, actionable tips you can use today. These are simple steps — nothing technical — that help you build good money habits and avoid common mistakes.

1. Start with a simple budget

  • Write down your monthly income and fixed expenses (rent, EMIs, utilities). Keep it to one page or a single note.
  • Set a simple rule: essentials (50%), savings & investments (30%), wants (20%). Adjust to your situation.
  • Review once a month and move any leftover into savings or investments.

2. Build an emergency fund first

  • Target 3–6 months of basic expenses as a safety buffer in a liquid account (savings or liquid funds).
  • Start small: even ₹2,000–5,000 per month adds up quickly.

3. Handle high-interest debt quickly

  • Focus repayments on credit cards and personal loans first — they usually cost the most.
  • Consider a balance transfer to a lower-rate card or consolidating multiple loans if it reduces total interest.

4. Use EMIs wisely

  • Match EMI tenure to the item: shorter tenures for fast-depreciating items (mobiles), longer for durable assets (house).
  • Keep EMI load comfortable: a good rule is to keep all EMIs below ~40% of take-home pay (less if you have other obligations).

5. Save before you spend (pay yourself first)

  • Automate a monthly transfer to a savings or investment account on payday.
  • Treat this as a fixed expense so you don't skip it when tempted to spend.

6. Start simple investments

  • For most beginners, monthly SIPs into a diversified mutual fund (equity or hybrid) are a good start.
  • Keep a lumpsum reserve in fixed deposits or liquid funds for short-term goals.

7. Count total ownership cost when buying big items

  • For cars and houses, include insurance, taxes, maintenance and fuel/utility costs — not just the sticker price.
  • Use HeroZero calculators to compare options: Car Affordability, House Affordability and Mobile Affordability (links below).

8. Use the right insurance cover

  • Health insurance and adequate life cover (if others depend on you) are priorities.
  • Keep policies simple and review annually — avoid too many small overlapping plans.

9. Mind taxes but keep it simple

  • Use tax-saving instruments sensibly (like ELSS, PPF, Section 80C options) if they fit your goals.
  • Don't choose an investment only for the tax benefit; first consider returns and liquidity.

10. Small habits that matter

  • Track subscriptions and cancel unused ones.
  • Compare prices for big purchases and wait for planned sales rather than impulse buys.
  • Cook at home more often and limit dining out to special occasions.

11. Quick checklist before any major purchase

  1. Do I need it or want it?
  2. Can I pay cash or do I need EMI? If EMI, can I afford the monthly payment comfortably?
  3. Have I accounted for additional costs (maintenance, insurance, taxes)?
  4. Will this purchase block other financial goals (emergency fund, investments)?

If you're unsure, keep it small and reversible

Prefer options that leave you flexibility — shorter EMIs, refundable deposits, trial periods for subscriptions. When in doubt, delay the decision for 24–72 hours and re-check.
